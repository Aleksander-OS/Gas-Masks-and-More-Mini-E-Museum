Shop scale

A shop scale manufactured by the Warsaw Scales Factory (SRW "MAR FAB" - Stowarzyszenie Rzemieślników Warszawskich "Marszałkowska Fabryka" [SRW "MAR FAB" - Association of Warsaw Craftsmen "Marshal facility") in 1942. The M.Meer visible above the date most likely indicates the person who was responsible for the creation of the scale itself. Interestingly, however, it was legalized for use only in (probably) 1988 (I'm not sure because of the fact that two stamps were placed on top of each other). The "Bucket" or "Beaker" visible in the first photo (because I don't know what to call it) is not original and was most likely used due to its similar weight to the plate (also not original, most likely cut and painted by hand). An interesting fact may be that it belonged to my great-grandfather, where he used it to trade, for example, raspberries, but also to buy various things (thanks to this, he could be more sure whether someone was trying to cheat him). Along with the scale, there are also weights, which I will describe in more detail below.

Orginal weights

As for the weights, I have found 9 so far, although from my father's stories I know that there should be porcelain ones somewhere. 2 of them come from the year of production of the scale, namely 1942 (although they may be older because the date of 1942 is the date of legalization, not production) they are made of brass. For information, markings with numbers are the date of production and those with e.g. "4RP4" are markings of the office where the scale/weights were legalized
