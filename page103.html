Cal.22lr ammunition boxes

But why would anyone need firearms in the Polish People's Republic? After all, in communist countries, civilians could not own weapons! well... well no, at least not in Poland. Although it may seem strange, obtaining a permit for a weapon firing integrated ammunition was possible, although extremely difficult, but for those who managed to do so, there also had to be ammunition, so it was. Of course, it was also exported to other countries, but it was nothing groundbreaking. These two boxes come from March 12, 1971 and March 25, 1971, respectively, and were produced by the NITRON chemical plant in Słupski Młyn.
